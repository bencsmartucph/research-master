Argument**The wrong politics problem:** Why do workers genuinely harmed by automation, trade exposure, and occupational decline so reliably support political movements whose programmes deepen their precarity?**The answer:** Welfare institutions are sorting mechanisms that determine the *direction* of political response, not merely its intensity. The operative variable is not how much welfare states spend but what they communicate about recipients' worth and citizenship status — specifically, whether they decommodify workers (CWED) or merely activate them (ALMP).**The three-stage damage cascade:**1. Conditional welfare damages the self-concept → identity switches from class to cultural2. Under cultural identity, economic grievance is misattributed to immigrants rather than structural change3. Defensive othering ("kicking down") reinforces and extends the scrounger narrative**The recursive loop:** Conditional welfare produces attitudes supporting further conditionality, which deepens the damage in the next iteration. Policy feedback running backwards.---## Key Datasets| Study | Folder | What it adds ||-------|--------|-------------|| Baccini (2024) | `data/raw/baccini_2024/` | Austerity → populism; analysis-ready files || Im et al.
