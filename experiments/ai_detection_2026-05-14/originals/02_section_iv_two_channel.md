A final scope condition concerns which channel of welfare-institutional effect this paper's cross-national design can identify. The mechanism developed in §III operates through two distinct pathways. An *encounter* channel runs through specific institutional interactions, in which welfare's allocation and judgement together damage or preserve a worker's self-concept; this is the canonical mechanism in Soss (1999), Wagner (2022), Patrick (2016), and Mettler (2011) on the submerged-state contrast. An *environment* channel operates on the broader polity: welfare design shapes the political vocabulary in which any worker, encounter-experienced or not, understands their own vulnerability and that of others. Cross-national variation in welfare design is dominated by the environment channel. Liberal-regime and Nordic-regime workers absorb sharply different welfare vocabularies through media, neighbourhood, family, and public discourse regardless of personal benefit claim, and the 15-country CWED finding is therefore best read as identifying the environment channel's asymmetric signature. The encounter channel is a sub-component the cross-national design cannot separately identify; its separation is the explicit object of the within-individual register-linked thesis follow-up indicated in §V.G. The asymmetric claim this paper sustains is that welfare *environment* runs more reliably toward damage than toward repair. Whether welfare *encounter* operates symmetrically, asymmetrically, or differently still is a question the present data cannot answer.
