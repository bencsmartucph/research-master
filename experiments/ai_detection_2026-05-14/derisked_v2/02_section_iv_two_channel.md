A final scope condition involves which channel of welfare-institutional effect our cross-national design can detect. The mechanism from §III operates through two distinct pathways. An encounter channel runs through specific institutional interactions. Welfare's allocation and judgement together damage or preserve a worker's self-concept; this is the canonical mechanism in Soss (1999), Wagner (2022), Patrick (2016), and Mettler (2011) on the submerged state contrast. An environment channel operates on the broader polity. Welfare design shapes the political vocabulary in which any worker understands their own vulnerability. This holds whether or not they have encounter experience. It also shapes how they perceive the vulnerability of others. Cross-national variation in welfare design is dominated by the environment channel. Liberal regime and Nordic regime workers absorb sharply different welfare vocabularies. These come through media, neighbourhood, family, and public discourse. Personal benefit claims are not required for this absorption. We therefore read the 15-country CWED finding as identifying the environment channel's asymmetric signature. The encounter channel is a sub-component our cross-national design cannot separately identify. Its separation is the explicit object of the within-individual register-linked thesis follow-up indicated in §V.G. The asymmetric claim we sustain here is that welfare environment runs more reliably toward damage than toward repair. Whether welfare encounter operates symmetrically, asymmetrically, or differently still remains open; the present data cannot answer this question.
