## Candidate extensions (ranked by Ben's stated curiosity × likely payoff)

1. **Annual CWED via Scruggs-Allan-Otto Comparative Welfare Entitlements Database.**
   Fixes the temporal mismatch (current CWED = mean 2005-2011; ESS outcomes 2012-2018). Enables time-varying CWED with country fixed effects — a materially stronger identification claim than the cross-sectional correlation. Feasibility probe first: does SAO have annual coverage through 2018 for the 15 sample countries?

2. **Recognition-deficit index (Steiner et al. 2023 four-facet model) from ESS.**
   Direct test of the recognition mechanism §III.E theorises, rather than the indirect "anti-immigration as evidence of damaged recognition" route. Feasibility probe: do the ESS rounds carry the four-facet items (economic-needs, work, service-access, opinion-expression recognition)?

3. **Status discordance (Kurer & van Staalduinen 2022 random-forest expected-status).**
   Test whether status discordance moderates RTI→anti-immigration by welfare regime. Highest diagnostic value for the sorting-vs-asymmetric question: if sorting drives it, discordance should absorb the variance CWED currently explains. Feasibility probe: is parental-background data in the ESS extract sufficient to predict expected status?

4. **Three-welfare-measures triangulation (Ennser-Jedenastik playbook).**
   CWED + Bertelsmann Social Justice + relative redistribution. Asymmetric pattern surviving all three strengthens; surviving only CWED weakens. Cheapest of the four.
