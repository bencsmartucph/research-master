### `two_channel_theory.md` (HIGHEST LEVERAGE — start here)

Develops the encounter-vs-environment distinction into a falsifiable theory with three predictions in increasing data-cost order. The seminar paper's data is dominated by the *environment channel* (welfare-as-political-vocabulary operating on the whole polity, encounter-experienced or not), not the *encounter channel* (welfare-as-direct-experience operating on recipients specifically). The puzzle the framing solves: cross-national CWED moderation is strong, within-country regional aggregate moderation is null, sub-component decomposition shows broad working-age salience with pensions decoupled — all three patterns cohere under environment-channel dominance, none under encounter-channel dominance.

### `care_without_connection.md`

Sharpens §III.E's third pillar (irreversibility of defensive othering) by naming what kind of political work welfare design alone cannot do.
