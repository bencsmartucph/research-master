## Overview

This paper explores how welfare institutions may shape social identity not just through direct economic effects, but by influencing moral frameworks about universalism versus particularism. This offers important insights for understanding how institutional variation across welfare states might mediate the relationship between economic insecurity and social identity formation.

---

## Key Relevance

The universalism vs. particularism framework connects to how welfare institutions shape moral reasoning:

- **Universalist** moral frameworks emphasize impartial treatment and equal concern for all
- **Particularist** moral frameworks emphasize loyalty to in-groups and differential treatment based on group membership

Welfare institutions that emphasize universal provision may promote universalist moral frameworks, while those that are more selective or conditional may reinforce particularist frameworks that distinguish between deserving and undeserving groups.
