# IMPLENTATION AND TYPES "Finding proactive features in labour market policies: A reflection based on the evidence" by Miguel Á. Malo, This research paper explores how labour market policies can become more proactive rather than merely reactive to economic shocks. The author defines "proactive" features as those that allow policies to pre-emptively address a wide range of labour market problems rather than simply reacting to specific issues. The paper primarily analyzes Active Labour Market Policies (ALMPs) but also considers how they can be complementary to "passive" income support policies.

## 2. Main Arguments and Theoretical Framework

**Central thesis and arguments**:

- Proactive and reactive features in labour market policies are complementary, but more proactive features are needed to confront future challenges.
- Implementation is a key channel for enhancing proactive features of labour market policies.
- The effectiveness of ALMPs varies significantly between developed and developing/emerging countries.
- Combinations of policies (such as activation strategies in developed countries and social protection floors in developing countries) can enhance proactive features.
- Sustained funding and administrative capacity are crucial for enhancing proactive characteristics.
- Social dialogue, particularly tripartite dialogue, can improve policy coordination and implementation of labour market policies.
- **Proactive labour market policy**: "A labour market policy has proactive features when this measure or intervention is not necessarily designed to react to a specific problem at a given moment in the short term, but to potentially pre-empt a wide range of problems or imbalances caused by economic shocks or other sources of unexpected changes." (p. 1)
- **Active Labour Market Policies (ALMPs)**: Policies aimed at increasing or improving employment opportunities for unemployed or inactive persons, including training, public works, employment subsidies, self-employment support, and labour market services.
- **Passive Labour Market Policies**: Policies focused on income maintenance when workers are out of employment, such as unemployment benefits and early retirement schemes.
- **Activation strategies**: "A combination of income support policies – mainly unemployment insurance and assistance – with different ALMPs, including different degrees of conditionality on job-search intensity." (p. 13)
- **Social protection floors**: A combination of policies that provide income and health security through cash transfers and access to essential social services, with an explicit anti-poverty objective.

## 4. Key Findings and Evidence

- Training programmes in developed countries show modest short-term impacts but clearly positive medium and long-term impacts. In developing countries, the impacts of training do not increase over time.
- Employment subsidies have mainly short-term impacts with little effect in the medium and long term, and are most effective when targeting specific groups.
- Self-employment and microenterprise programmes show strong evidence of effectiveness in both developed and developing economies, especially for disadvantaged groups.
- Direct job creation in the public sector is generally less effective than other ALMPs in developed countries but may have greater promise in developing countries.
- Gender differences exist, with larger impacts for women compared to men in many programmes.
- Youth-specific programmes typically have lower impacts than mixed-age programmes in developed countries but show better results in middle and low-income countries.
- "Policy implementation is found to be a key issue that has an impact on the efficacy of specific ALMPs and their aggregate impacts, and on the effects of the integrated approach between ALMPs and income support policies." (p. 1)
- Self-employment and microenterprise programmes show strong evidence of effectiveness in both developed and developing economies, especially for disadvantaged groups.
- "The combined approach, implementing ALMPs jointly with income support and basic services such as education and health, is aimed to target specifically the poorest households." (p. 18)

## 5. Policy Implications and Mechanisms

- Enhance proactive features in labour market policies through better implementation, adequate funding, and administrative capacity.
- Maintain continuity of public spending on ALMPs throughout the business cycle, not just during recessions.
- Use tripartite social dialogue to improve specific and aggregate effectiveness of labour market policies.
- Develop balanced portfolios of labour market policies with both proactive and reactive features.
- In developing countries, combine ALMPs with income support and basic services to strengthen social protection floors.
- In developed countries, integrate ALMPs with income support policies through activation strategies.
- Support self-employment and micro-enterprise creation, which show clear proactive features in both developed and developing countries.
