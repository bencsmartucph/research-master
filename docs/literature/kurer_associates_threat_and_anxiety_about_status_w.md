# Kurer associates threat and anxiety about status with ‘the develop‑ ment of socially conservative or even authoritarian character traits’, and threats to the ‘perceived value’ of some forms of work and those that perform them with increased hostility to groups ‘seen as competitors in the social hierarchy’, as a means of achieving a relative stabilisation of their own status and value (Kurer 2020, p. 1805). Because social status is relative, these work‑ ers can shore up their status by ‘maintaining their social distance’ (Im and Komp‑Leukkunen 2021, p. 458) from other groups and individuals perceived as occupying a lower status in the social order (see also Gidron and Hall 2019).
